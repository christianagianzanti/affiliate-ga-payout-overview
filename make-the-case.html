Pay affiliates on the gross-adds they drive — and pay them sooner.

Today Shopify pays affiliate commissions on a long-delayed full-price event. We propose moving to a Paid Trial / GA (gross-add) payout: commission on the metric the team is already goaled on, paid up-front, protected by a clawback.

The problem

Shopify has the longest payout period in the competitive affiliate landscape. Partners wait — cash they could be recycling into acquisition sits undelivered, and the thing we pay them for isn't the thing we measure them on. Note that gross-add is a unit we already budget and measure in. Paying partners on GA doesn't invent a new metric — it pays on the one we're already goaled on.

Why now — three lines of evidence

01

Competitive gap

We're the slowest payer in the market. A GA payout aligns commissions to the metric the team is goaled on and moves us to parity.

The logic in one line

Pay on gross-adds→ pay up-front→ partners reinvest earlier in paid / PPC→ more acquisition

The guardrail that makes up-front safe

🛡️

Clawback. 20 days after a paid trial is paid, if the shop cancelled or froze, the up-front commission is reversed back off the partner — so we pay early without paying for churn. Rolls out first with the 5 managed pay-on-trial partners, and skips trials already reversed.

What we're asking for

Endorsement to advance the GA-payout model from case-building into rollout planning — rate modeling, fraud protections, comms, and Growth Center updates are already in flight; the remaining gate is leadership approval.
