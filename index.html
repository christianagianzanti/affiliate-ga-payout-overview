Hack Days 40 · Affiliate Paid Trial-Payout Viability

Moving affiliate commissions onto a Paid Trial / GA payout

Shopify runs the longest payout period in the competitive affiliate landscape. This initiative builds the case for paying partners on the gross-add (GA) they drive — sooner, aligned to the metric the team is goaled on — instead of waiting out the long-delayed full-price payout.

The Case Rate Modeling & Cost Impact Clawback Fraud & Risk Open Questions
TL;DR
Paying affiliates on the gross-adds they drive — instead of the long-delayed full-price payout — is a win on both sides. For partners: faster cash flow to reinvest, payout timing on par with competitors, a simpler GTM story, and a reason for lapsed creators to come back. For Shopify: commissions tied directly to the metric we’re goaled on, faster recruit activation, and a halo effect as happy partners refer new ones.
01

The case

Why a Paid Trial / GA payout wins — for partners and for Shopify

  • ✓Competitive parity on payout timing. Aligns our payout timeframe with competitors instead of trailing the market.
  • ✓Earlier cash flow for affiliates. Partners can reinvest sooner and scale through content and paid ads.
  • ✓Sustains affiliate affinity for Shopify. Incentivizes partners to keep and grow their loyalty to Shopify.
  • ✓Rewards top-of-funnel influence. Pays on how quality creators actually drive, eliminating noisy downstream conversions they can’t control — especially for partners with no levers to nurture referrals.
  • ✓Win back lapsed partners. Re-engage affiliates who migrated to other platforms because of the extended payout window.
  • ✓Simpler, clearer GTM. Easier for partners to digest, and easier for new recruits to conceptualize their forecasted success from activation.
  • ✓Halo effect & word of mouth. Existing affiliates’ happiness translates into testimonials that pull in new recruits.
  • ✓Commissions aligned to our goaled metric. Pays directly on the primary metric we’re goaled on.
  • ✓Faster activation for recruits. Shorter path from signup to first payout.

How GA rates are set, what we honor through the transition, and what it costs

Theory behind the model

Start from what we pay each partner today, express it against their cohorted gross-adds (GA), and round down to the nearest $5 to set the GA rate. Rounding down keeps the program conservative while keeping partners roughly whole through the transition.

Full Price is honored through the tail. Any Free Trial logged before the Oct 1, 2026 transition stays on the old Full Price model — we keep commissioning those shops as they mature to full price for up to 125 days, so the last pre-transition cohort pays out around early Feb 2027. Free Trials logged from Oct 1 onward pay on the GA model.

03

Clawback mechanism

Explained by Engineering

The clawback event fires 20 days after a paid trial is paid: if the shop has cancelled or frozen, the up-front commission is reversed back off the partner.

  • •Scope: starts with the 5 managed pay-on-trial partners
  • •Skips trials that have already been reversed (no double clawback)
